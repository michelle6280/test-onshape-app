Cup

Try reading the perspective drawing on the right and attempt to solve this problem. If you're stuck, scroll down to find a detailed tutorial.

Goals:

This page will teach you how to sketch and revolve to create a cup. You will be introduced to Line, Revolve (a face/sketch about an axis), Plane (adding a new plane), Sweep (a face or edge), and the Appearance Panel (where you can add color to different parts in a Part Studio.

Sketch the cross-sectional area for half of the cup:

Start out with a new Part Studio Sketch, and pick a plane to sketch on.

Use the Line tool, located on the left in the tool bar, to roughly draw the cross-section of half of your cup. Make sure to connect all of the lines to each other.

** Note: When we revolve this sketch, we want to revolve it around the center axis, so make sure that you have a vertical line (part of the sketch) that runs through the center point. Therefore, when we use the Revolve function, we can revolve around this vertical line.

Dimension your cup (optional)

Make your cup more exact by using the Dimension tool.

You can dimension your cup however you want - it depends on how thick you want your cup. In our case, we set it to 0.4 inches.

Revolve sketch

To actually create the cup, we can use the Revolve function, located on the left in the tool bar.

Select your cross-sectional area sketch as the sketch region to revolve, and then click on the vertical axis that passes through the center point, which you drew, as the revolve axis.

** Remember that we intentionally drew a vertical line to act as the vertical axis. You can start to make sense of why we drew that - it also acts as the bottom of the cup.

Then click the green check button to finish the Revolve.

Create another plane

To make a handle, we have to sketch on the side of the revolved cup. We will create another plane, which is located on the right in the tool bar.

** Note: We can't sketch right on the Revolve that we just performed because that sketch is on a different plane. Therefore, we create a new plane that is offset from the plane at the origin since we want our cup handle to be on the edge of our cup instead of in the middle.

Offset the plane near to the edge of the cup, or wherever you want the top of the cup handle to start.

Sketch a circle for the handle

We will sketch a circle on the plane that we just created. This circle is essentially the cross-sectional area of the handle.

** Make sure you sketch the circle in the area where the plane intersects the side of the cup because we want the handle to be attached to the cup.

You may dimension this circle if you want with the Dimension tool, but it is not necessary.

Sketch the path of the handle

We want to sketch the path in which the handle will go. To do this, we can use the 3 point arc tool, located on the left in the tool bar.

** Note: We do this because we will be using the Sweep tool, which requires an initial sketch and the path which it will follow through.

After selecting the 3 point arc tool, click once on the center of the circle that we just made, once near the bottom of the cup where the handle will end, and then once in between the first two points but outside of the revolved cup to give the handle a curve.

Sweep to create the handle

We will use the Sweep function, which is located on the left in the tool bar.

Select the circle as the sketch curve to sweep. Then select the 3 point arc that you just sketched as the sweep path.

Click the green check to finish the sweep.

Give your cup some color! (optional)

Your cup is all done, but you might think that it looks a little boring. We will use the Appearance Panel to change the colors.

** Note: The cup itself and the handle are two separate parts because the cup is a part while the handle is just a surface that was modified with Sweep. So, in the Appearance Panel, there will be two options to change colors.

There are different ways you can change the color, such as the Palette or the Mixer.
