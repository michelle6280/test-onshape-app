This is an example using SKETCH and EXTRUDE ADD/REMOVE.

Step 1: Sketch a 2" x 2" square

Start out with a new Part Studio Sketch, which you can add by clicking on the "+" button located at the bottom left corner of the screen.

At the origin, draw a Center Point Rectangle by going to the tool bar on the top and then clicking on the icon with a rectangle.

Then click once on the origin, move your mouse outward, and click again to stop the sketch.

** Note: Every time you draw a new sketch, Onshape prompts you to define a plane to draw the sketch on. It's like picking which side of a sheet of paper you will be drawing on. **

On the tool bar, we will use the Dimension tool to specify the lengths of the sides. Click once on a side of the rectangle and move your mouse outward. Click again to adjust the length to 2in.

Do the same for the other side of the rectangle to create a square.

Step 2: Extrude add a face

To create a cube, we want to use the Extrude function, which can be found on the tool bar.

** Note: There are two ways you can use the Extrude function: **

1) Click Extrude while you are still in Sketch 1 (the sketch of the square from before) or

2) Click the check mark to finish Sketch 1, click Extrude, and then click on Sketch 1 to specify what you are extruding

To make the square into a cube, we want to Add an extrude. Since this is our first extrude to this sketch, we are creating a New extrude.

We can change the Depth of the extrude (or height of the cube) to 2in.

Step 3: Sketch a circle with a diameter of 1in

To make a hole, we need to start with a sketch of a circle, which can be found in the tool bar.

** Remember again that Onshape will always prompt you to define a plane where the sketch will be drawn, so click on any plane that you want to extrude a hole through. **

** For a better view, you can change your perspective view to the plane you chose to draw a sketch on. Ex: top view for sketching on the top plane **

Click on the circle icon in the tool bar and draw a Center Point Circle by clicking once on the origin, moving your mouse outward, and then clicking again to stop the sketch.

We can use the Dimension tool again to specify the length of the diameter by clicking on the rim of the circle (circumference). Set this to 1in.

Step 4: Extrude remove a hole

To actually make a hole, we want to use the Extrude function again, but instead of adding an extrude, we want to remove an extrude.

Set the depth to 2in because we want the hole to go all the way through our cube.

** Note: You can only Extrude Remove a sketch if there already is an extruded part. In other words, you cannot start off with an Extrude Remove before making your cube. **
