Dog Collar

Introduction

Create a dog collar in Onshape! This dog collar was created in Onshape using several part studios and a few basic tools.

You try!

To start, create a new sketch by clicking the "Sketch" button in your Onshape project. Select the top plane, either by clicking "Top plane" in the left sidebar or clicking on the top plane in the workspace. Rotate the view cube in the top right corner so that the top face is displayed head-on.

Step 1: Draw the circles

Create the round shape of the collar by drawing two circles. To draw the outer circle, click on the circle tool with the new sketch still in editing mode. Click on the origin with the circle tool, and drag outwards until the circle has your desired diameter. Once the circle is the proper size, release your cursor, and the circle will be complete.

Draw the inner circle with its center at the origin again. The inner circle should have a slightly smaller diameter than the outer circle (about .5 inches less).

Step 2: Extrude the collar

Begin a new extrusion by clicking the "extrude" icon in the toolbar. Then select the space between the inner and outer circle. This will automatically give your collar a depth of 1 inch. You can change the depth of the sketch in the menu that appears when you edit the extrusion.

Step 3: Change the color of the collar

Right click on any part of the collar and select "edit appearance." In the color selection menu, select the color of your choice. Click the green check when you are satisfied with your selection.

Step 4: Create a ring

Create a new part studio by clicking on the "+" icon at the bottom left corner of the screen. If you wish to name the part studios according to the parts they contain, you do this by right clicking on the part studio tabs. Pivot your screen to see the front plane head-on. Create a new sketch in the front plane.

Within the sketch, draw an arc by clicking where you'd like to place the starting point of the arc, the end of the arc, and the radius of the arc. The distance between the ends of the arc should be at least 1 inch.

Step 5: Create a tag

Create a new part studio. Pivot your screen to see the front plane head-on. Create a new sketch in the front plane. In the sketch, use the line, spline, and mirror (if symmetrical) tools to create a tag with the shape of your choice. Extrude the tag by a very small amount to make it thin and choose a color.

Step 6: Assembly

Click on the tab at the bottom of your screen labeled "Assembly 1." In this empty workspace, click on the "Insert" button in the toolbar, which is roughly where "Sketch" would be located in a part studio tab. In this dropdown menu, select each piece you have created so far: the collar, the ring, and the tag.

Once all parts are in your assembly workspace, begin to drag the parts together to resemble a fully-formed dog collar.

If you select each part, you can use the arrows to drag the parts to your desired positioning. You may need to resize some of the parts back in the part studios in order for them to fit together.

Your dog collar is complete!
